Welfare Valuation of an EU Farrowing Crate Ban

AQALY Framework Application: Carpenter & Kanepajs, 2026

This page applies the AQALY-4PD-9L framework (the Animal Quality-Adjusted Life Year with 4 Physical Domains assessed on a 9-Level welfare scale; Espinosa, Browning & Treich, 2025) to quantify and monetise the sow welfare difference between farrowing crates and free-farrowing systems.

Domain-by-domain assessment

Nutrition
Crate 5 · Free farrowing 5
Lactation diets are nutritionally similar across housing systems. Both receive standard commercial rations formulated for peak milk output. No meaningful difference in nutritional welfare; both scored as neutral.
Environment
Crate 9: Extreme spatial restriction confines the sow to a metal frame (~0.6 × 2.1 m) with no turning possible, limited to standing and lying. Bare or slatted flooring with no manipulable substrate. Thermoregulation severely compromised (unable to move to cooler/warmer zones), poor air quality from ammonia exposure, inadequate lighting regimes, and noise stress from neighbouring sows. The worst-scoring domain for the crate system.
Free farrowing 6: Indoor pen (~6.5 m²) with deep straw bedding and freedom of movement. Thermoregulation possible through postural and positional choice. Sensory diversity from bedding manipulation, more natural light/dark cycle exposure. Still industrial single-sow housing with hard boundaries; basic spatial and thermal needs are met, but limited enrichment variety and no outdoor access keep this below neutral.
Health
Crate 7: Shoulder ulcers from prolonged recumbency on hard surfaces, urinary tract infections, lameness, and muscle atrophy. Stereotypic bar-biting from frustration. Physiological stress markers elevated.
Free farrowing 4: Pressure injuries largely eliminated; improved musculoskeletal condition from regular movement. Above-neutral welfare: the free-farrowing system actively reduces injury risk, with minimal or no skin lesions observed in deep-straw pens.
Behavioural Interactions
Crate 8: Complete inability to perform nest-building (the sow's strongest pre-farrowing motivation). No maternal choice over piglet contact; interaction timing forced by physical structure. Prevention of locomotion leads to development of stereotypies (bar-biting, sham-chewing). No escape from distressing stimuli possible. Social interaction with other sows entirely prevented.
Free farrowing 5: Nest-building possible with straw provision; sow controls piglet interaction and can express core maternal behaviours. Neutral overall: while nest-building material is provided, full expression still constrained by indoor single-sow pen design. Social investigation of environment enabled, escape and avoidance behaviour possible, reduced stereotypy expression.

AQALY Scores

Each domain grade maps to a coefficient from Table A2 (Espinosa et al. 2025, merged sample N=1,062, PCHIP interpolation). These coefficients represent the welfare decrement relative to grade 1 (best possible).

Coefficients are welfare decrements estimated via discrete choice experiments and time-trade-off tasks. Grade 1 = 0 (reference); grade 9 = most severe. Higher absolute values mean worse welfare in that domain.

Willingness-to-Pay Comparison

Consumer willingness-to-pay (WTP) for pig welfare improvements provides an independent cross-check on the AQALY-derived values. Two meta-analyses anchor the literature:

  • Clark et al. (2017): 54 studies, 335 WTP estimates, 17 countries (56% EU). Found positive WTP across all species, with pigs receiving the lowest premium of any livestock species. Northern EU countries showed lower WTP, attributed to higher trust in existing regulation.
  • Lagerkvist & Hess (2011): 24 studies, 106 estimates. Found 50–150% stated premiums for welfare improvements, but noted substantial hypothetical bias inflating stated over revealed preferences.

Approximate conversion to the sow-crate-day unit used in this assessment:

  • Eurobarometer-based (own assumption): “Six in ten respondents (60%) are willing to pay more for products sourced from animal welfare-friendly farming systems” (Special Eurobarometer 533, 2023). We assume a 5% retail price premium for improved sow welfare. 5% × EUR 6/kg × 1,800 kg retail pork per sow per year ÷ 69 crate-days = ~EUR 7.8 per sow-crate-day
  • German Tierwohl levy (revealed preference): EUR 0.0625/kg × 1,800 kg ÷ 69 days = ~EUR 1.6 per sow-crate-day

The assumed-WTP estimate of ~EUR 7.8 per sow-crate-day converges with the AQALY Neuron count estimate of EUR 9.06 per sow-crate-day. This convergence between a top-down stated-preference approach and a bottom-up welfare-science approach is notable, though the WTP assumption carries wide uncertainty and is not specific to farrowing crate removal.

Methodology

▶ AQALY Framework

The Animal Quality-Adjusted Life Year (AQALY) framework was developed by Espinosa, Browning, and Treich (2025). It adapts the human QALY concept for animal welfare valuation using a discrete choice experiment (DCE) with time trade-off (TTO) methodology.

The 4PD-9L variant used here assesses four physical domains (Nutrition, Environment, Health, Behaviour) on a nine-grade scale. Coefficients were estimated from a merged sample of N=1,062 respondents. Domain grades for this assessment were translated from a Five Domains analysis by Marie Reinke.

Formula: AQALY/year = 1 + βNutrition + βEnvironment + βHealth + βBehaviour

The resulting AQALY difference is pro-rated to a 28-day lactation block and monetised at the EU WTP threshold for a human QALY (EUR 147,000), scaled by a moral weight.

▶ Moral Weight Choices

WTP / Eurobarometer (EUR 7.8/SCD): Special Eurobarometer 533 (2023) found 60% of EU citizens willing to pay more for welfare-friendly products; 26% would pay up to 5% more. We assume a 5% retail price premium for improved sow welfare. Converted: 5% × EUR 6/kg × 1,800 kg retail pork per sow per year ÷ 69 crate-days = ~EUR 7.8 per sow-crate-day. Methodologically distinct from the AQALY framework: a top-down stated-preference approach rather than bottom-up welfare science. Its convergence with the Neuron count estimate (EUR 9.06) is notable.

Neuron count (1/44 = 0.0227): The ratio of cortical neurons between pig (~2.2 billion) and human (~86 billion, of which cortical ~16 billion) brains. A conservative lower bound that treats raw neuron count as the sole determinant of moral weight.

Welfare Range (0.515): The Rethink Priorities Welfare Range estimate, which aggregates across multiple theories of consciousness and moral status. Substantially higher, reflecting that many ethical frameworks assign pigs considerably more than neuron-proportional moral weight.

These three approaches span the plausible range. WTP and Neuron count converge near EUR 8-9 per sow-crate-day; the Welfare Range estimate is an order of magnitude higher, reflecting ethical frameworks that assign pigs substantially more moral weight than neuron proportionality alone implies.

▶ Economic Cost Source

The economic cost of EUR 0.22 per sow-crate-day comes from the EUFF economic module, which estimates the net producer cost of transitioning from farrowing crates to free-farrowing systems. This figure integrates capital costs (pen conversion), operating cost changes (labour, feed, mortality), and productivity effects across the EU-27 herd.

EUR 136 per sow per year and EUR 0.06 per kg of pork are derived from the same module using standard denominators (2.3 litters/year, EU-average output per sow).

▶ Limitations
  • Domain grades are preliminary, pending validation by animal welfare scientists. Grade assignments involve judgment calls in translating Five Domains qualitative assessments to the AQALY numeric scale.
  • The AQALY framework captures physical domains only. Mental/affective states beyond those expressed through physical domains are not directly measured.
  • Moral weight is the most consequential parameter. Moving from the Neuron count floor to the Welfare Range estimate multiplies all values by a factor of 22.7.
  • The EUR 147,000 QALY threshold reflects EU-average health-economic valuation; national thresholds vary.
  • The comparison assumes 28 continuous crate-days per litter. Temporary-crating systems (confinement for a subset of days) would reduce the delta proportionally.
